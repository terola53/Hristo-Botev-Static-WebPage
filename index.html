Hristo Botev

Bulgarian revolutionary and poet

"He who falls in the battle for freedom does not die..."

Here's a timeline of Hristo Botev

  • 1847 - Born in Kalofer
  • (1857-1858) - Goes to study in Karlovo and eventually comes back to Kalofer
  • 1863 -Continues his education under the guidance of his father and graduates in the Kalofer school
  • October the same year - Goes to study in Russia but is expelled in 1865
  • 1867 - Returns in Kalofer, he began to preach a revolt against the the Turks, after which he finally left Kalofer
  • 1867 - At that time in the newspaper "Gaida" was published the first poem of Hristo Botev - "Mayce si"
  • 1872 - He was arrested for conspiratorial revolutionary activities and sent to Focsani Prison, but was released due to the intercession of Levski and Karavelov
  • 1875 - Together with Stefan Stambolov, he published the collection of poems "Songs and Poems"
  • 1876 - Following the news of the April Uprising, Botev began to organize a detachment and become it's leader
  • 1876 - The last heavy battle - in the evening after the battle a bullet pierced Botev
